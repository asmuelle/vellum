# Vellum

> A private family medical-record vault that photographs lab results, prescriptions, and discharge papers, then extracts, trends, and explains them — unlimited capture, and not one page ever uploaded.

**Category:** Edge AI / on-device inference (iOS + Android) 

## Concept

A private family medical-record vault that photographs lab results, prescriptions, and discharge papers, then extracts, trends, and explains them — unlimited capture, and not one page ever uploaded.

## Target User & Payer

The sandwich generation (35-60) managing their own records plus aging parents' paperwork across multiple providers; chronic-condition patients tracking labs over years. The caregiver is the payer and the upsell engine.

## Why Edge AI Is Structural (not decoration)

AFM 3 Core Advanced image input plus the free OCRTool parse photographed labs, EOBs, prescriptions, and discharge summaries; @Generable extracts typed records (analyte, value, reference range, date) into longitudinal trend charts; Spotlight local RAG powers 'ask your records' Q&A ('when was Dad's last tetanus shot?'); AFM 3 generates plain-English explanations. Android: ML Kit GenAI Prompt API (text+image) on flagships, multimodal Gemma 3n E4B via LiteRT-LM elsewhere. Structural: medical documents are the canonical never-cloud data — the vault premise collapses if pages transit a server, unlimited scanning is uneconomic for cloud-vision rivals but free here, and 5.1.2(i) forces cloud competitors into ugly consent dialogs this app never shows.

## Why Now (2026 timing)

On-device vision for third-party apps became possible at WWDC26 (AFM 3 image input + OCRTool); mobile local OCR+LLM extraction has zero polished commercial players today; Health & Fitness passed $4B IAP and health-data breach headlines have pre-sold the distrust of cloud record apps.

## Proposed Monetization

$4.99/mo or $39.99/yr individual; $69.99/yr family plan (5 profiles) plus a $129 lifetime-family SKU made safe by zero inference COGS — a cloud competitor pays per page forever. 120K family-weighted subscribers at ~$50 blended ≈ $6M ARR.

## Competition & Gap

Apple Health Records covers only US provider integrations with no document parsing or Q&A; Fasten Health and cloud 'AI health record' startups require uploads (trust and 5.1.2(i) liability); CamScanner-class scanners do OCR with no medical structure, trends, or RAG. First-mover window before the ecosystem recompiles for the new vision APIs.

# Evaluation (multi-agent adversarial review)

## Monetization Analysis — score 6.5/10

This sits squarely in the 6-8 band: a plausible large market with a real wedge, but an unproven payer in this exact subcategory. Verified positives: (1) the payer exists and is motivated — AARP 2025 counts 63M US caregivers, 29% of them sandwich-generation, spending ~$10K/yr out of pocket, so $69.99/yr for a family vault is rounding error against demonstrated spend; (2) scan-to-subscription monetization is proven at scale (CamScanner alone clears an estimated ~$8M/month across stores per Sensor Tower) and Health & Fitness IAP passed $4B globally in 2025; (3) the claimed competitive gap largely checks out — Fasten Health is early-stage (~$20K disclosed funding, open-source freemium) and Apple Health Records does no document parsing or Q&A. Verified negatives that cap the score: the lab-parsing niche is NOT empty — Carrot Care scans and trends labs with a free core, Guava connects 50K+ provider portals on a free base tier, VitalDot already does multi-profile family lab tracking — so the free-core competitors create real pricing pressure, even if all are cloud-based. The deeper structural risk is the PHR graveyard pattern (Google Health, HealthVault): personal health record apps historically die from episodic engagement, not from privacy concerns, and 'never uploaded' is a marketing edge consumers rarely pay a premium for on its own. The on-device zero-COGS economics genuinely enable unlimited scanning and a safe lifetime SKU, which is a defensible cost wedge versus per-page cloud-vision rivals, but the $6M ARR target (120K paying families) is roughly 2-3x what comparable VC-backed apps in this niche appear to achieve; $2-4M ARR is the realistic 3-year ceiling. Medical OCR extraction errors on reference ranges also carry trust/liability risk that free competitors with portal API feeds (structured FHIR data) avoid.

## Recommended Revenue Model

Lead with the family plan, not the individual tier: $69.99/yr family (5 profiles) as the hero SKU, $39.99/yr ($4.99/mo) individual, and raise the lifetime-family SKU from $129 to $179 — zero inference COGS makes lifetime safe, but $129 is under 2x annual and caps LTV too early. Free tier: unlimited scanning and storage (the structural advantage cloud rivals can't match), with trends, multi-profile, and ask-your-records Q&A behind the paywall — the caregiver hits the paywall the moment they add a parent's profile, which is the natural conversion event. Justification: Carrot Care and Guava give away single-profile tracking, so the individual tier will face race-to-free pressure; the family/caregiver tier is where willingness to pay is demonstrated (~$10K/yr caregiving spend, AARP) and where no free competitor bundles parsing + trends + Q&A across profiles. Realistic targets: 30-60K paying family-weighted subscribers at ~$55 blended by year 3 = $1.7-3.3M ARR; the pitched $6M requires top-decile execution. Add a B2B2C channel later (eldercare agencies, employers with caregiving benefits) since per-seat COGS is zero.

## Adversarial Review — strongest case AGAINST (verdict: weakened)

(1) ACCURACY IS THE PRODUCT, AND 3B-CLASS MODELS CAN'T CARRY IT. Vellum's value is numerically trustworthy extraction from photographed paper, and that is exactly where small on-device models are weakest. Published clinical-extraction results show fine-tuned small LLMs topping out around ~90% per-field exact match, and benchmark work (MedRepBench) documents OCR-error propagation causing factual hallucinations — including misclassifying normal results as abnormal. At 90% per-field accuracy, a 20-analyte CBC/CMP panel has a ~88% chance of at least one wrong value; a transposed potassium or a missed decimal in a longitudinal trend chart is actively dangerous, not merely annoying. AFM 3 is ~3B-class with a small context window; multi-page discharge summaries and dense faxed lab tables exceed it, and handwritten prescriptions are out of reach of Vision OCR entirely. The only responsible design is a human-confirmation screen for every extracted value — which converts 'magic unlimited capture' into data-entry proofreading and guts the one-liner. @Generable guarantees schema shape, not truth. (2) THE 'NOT ONE PAGE EVER UPLOADED' PREMISE CONTRADICTS THE CORE USE CASE. The target user is a caregiver syncing Dad's records across the family. Pages must transit servers to reach the sister's phone: at best E2E CloudKit (technically 'uploaded', so the headline claim becomes marketing fiction), and to reach an Android parent there is no Apple path at all — the team must run its own encrypted relay, reintroducing the server, the liability, and the COGS that supposedly made the $129 lifetime SKU safe. (3) THE MOAT IS THREE MONTHS OLD AND SHARED. AFM 3 image input + OCRTool + Spotlight RAG were announced June 9, 2026 and ship to consumers in September — to every developer simultaneously, with the framework going open source and the new LanguageModel protocol making on-device vs Claude/Gemini a one-line swap (commoditizing the 'edge' stance itself). Apple shelved its full AI doctor in Feb 2026 but is shipping Health-data suggestions 'as early as this year' and has been rumored to partner with Quest/Labcorp; the explain-your-labs layer is a prime WWDC27 sherlock target, leaving only the paper-vault scanning niche. (4) THE FAMILY PLAN'S DEVICES CAN'T RUN THE PRODUCT. Apple Intelligence requires iPhone 15 Pro or later; ML Kit Prompt API multimodal is flagship-only (best on Pixel 10); Gemma 3n E4B does not fit comfortably in mid-range Android RAM. Aging parents — three of the five family-plan profiles — disproportionately own exactly the hardware this app cannot run on. (5) DISTRIBUTION IS UNSOLVED. 'Medical records' App Store queries are owned by free MyChart and insurer apps; privacy is a feature that doesn't screenshot; CAC against a $40-70/yr blended LTV doesn't close without an Apple feature slot, which is a hope, not a channel. (6) REGULATORY EDGE: plain-English 'explanations' of lab values from a 3B model walks the FDA CDS-exemption line and invites App Review 1.4.1 scrutiny; one screenshot of the app calling an abnormal value normal is an existential PR event. WHY IT NONETHELESS ISN'T DEAD: the niche (photographed-paper parsing + multi-profile family caregiving) is genuinely unserved — Apple Health Records does US provider APIs only and has repeatedly declined paper ingestion; the zero-marginal-cost economics vs per-page cloud-vision rivals are real; and an iOS-first version with a verification UX is shippable by 2-3 engineers on APIs that exist as of yesterday.

## Recommended Tech Stack

iOS (iOS 26 baseline, iOS 27 for AFM 3 image input, Sept 2026): VisionKit document camera (VNDocumentCameraViewController) for capture; Vision RecognizeDocumentsRequest for table-aware OCR; FoundationModels AFM 3 with @Generable typed schemas (analyte/value/unit/refRange/date) fed OCR TEXT, not raw pixels — use the built-in OCRTool and Spotlight search tool for local RAG Q&A; train a task-specific LoRA adapter with Apple's Foundation Models adapter toolkit for lab extraction; deterministic layer FIRST — regex/table parsers for the top US lab formats (Quest, Labcorp, hospital Epic printouts) with the LLM as fallback, plus a mandatory per-value human-confirmation UI; unit normalization (mg/dL vs mmol/L) in code, never in the model; GRDB/SQLite with NSFileProtectionComplete; NLContextualEmbedding (or EmbeddingGemma converted to Core ML) for the local vector index; Swift Charts for trends; CloudKit with Advanced Data Protection for E2E family sync (and honest 'end-to-end encrypted, we can never read it' copy instead of 'never uploaded'). Android: ML Kit Document Scanner + Text Recognition v2 for OCR; ML Kit GenAI Prompt API (Gemini Nano, image+text) on Pixel 8+/Galaxy S24+ AICore devices; Gemma 3n E2B (not E4B — mid-range RAM) via MediaPipe LLM Inference / LiteRT-LM as the fallback tier, text-only extraction path on devices that can't hold the vision encoder; EmbeddingGemma via LiteRT for RAG embeddings; SQLCipher + Android Keystore. Cross-platform family sync: ship iOS-only family plans in v1; if Android profiles are required, an Automerge/CRDT document store over a dumb zero-knowledge blob relay (client-held keys, libsodium), priced into COGS honestly. Explanations layer: template-grounded output (model fills slots against the verified structured record, cites the reference range shown on-screen) to stay inside the FDA CDS exemption.
