The on-device promise

Medical records are the data that should never touch the cloud.

So Vellum doesn't put them there. Your documents are read, parsed, and understood entirely on your device. Nothing transits a server — there's no server to transit. Cloud-vision tools charge per page forever and quietly keep a copy; Vellum does neither.

  • On-device OCR and deterministic parsers — no document leaves your phone.
  • Encrypted at rest and excluded from cloud backups by default.
  • Zero inference cost means scanning is unlimited — and lifetime pricing is honest.

Questions

The two questions everyone asks first.

Does anything get uploaded?
No. Vellum reads and parses your documents entirely on your device using on-device OCR and deterministic parsers. No page, image, or value is ever sent to a server — there is no server in the loop. Your vault is encrypted on the device and excluded from cloud backups by default.
Is this medical advice?
No. Vellum is a tool for organizing, trending, and understanding your own records — a vault and a record-keeper, not a doctor. Plain-English notes give general context about what a value measures; they are not diagnosis, treatment, or medical advice. Always talk to a qualified healthcare professional about your health.
Why do I have to confirm every value?
Because your records should be trustworthy, not guessed. Every extracted number is shown beside its source image and saved only after you approve it. That human-in-the-loop step is the whole point — you're never relying on a black box for something this important.
Is scanning really unlimited?
Yes. Because all parsing runs locally, there's no per-page cost to pass on to you. Cloud-vision services charge for every page forever; Vellum doesn't, so you can scan as much as you need on any plan.
What kinds of documents can I file?
Lab reports, prescriptions, and discharge summaries are the core. You can capture them for any family profile you set up — yourself, your parents, your partner, or your kids — and keep records from multiple providers together in one organized archive.
